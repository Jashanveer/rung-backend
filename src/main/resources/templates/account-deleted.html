Science-backed habit tips to take with you

  • 🧱

    Stack your habits

    Anchor new behaviours to existing ones. "After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for 2 minutes." The existing cue does the triggering for you.

  • 📏

    Make it stupidly small

    James Clear calls it the 2-minute rule: shrink the habit until it takes less than 2 minutes. A habit you actually do is infinitely more valuable than one you aspire to do.

  • 🔗

    Never miss twice

    Missing once is human. Missing twice is the start of a new habit — the habit of missing. One bad day doesn't define your trajectory; your response to it does.

  • 🌍

    Identity beats outcomes

    Don't say "I want to run a marathon." Say "I am a runner." Every habit completed is a vote for the identity you want. Cast enough votes and the identity becomes real.

  • 🎯

    Design your environment

    Put the book on your pillow. Leave the running shoes by the door. Make the good choice the obvious choice — willpower is a finite resource, environment design is not.

"You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems. Build better systems."

— James Clear, Atomic Habits
